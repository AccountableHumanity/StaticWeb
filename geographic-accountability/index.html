Geographic Accountability

Location Matters on the Internet

When someone leaves a review for a local business, comments on a local news story, or weighs in on local politics, there is an implicit assumption that they are actually from the area. But on today's internet, there is no way to verify that.

Anyone can claim to be from anywhere. A bot farm in one country can post reviews about restaurants in another. A coordinated campaign can flood a city council's comment section with opinions from people who have never set foot in the city. Foreign actors can masquerade as local voices to influence elections, public opinion, and community decisions.

GPS-Anchored Identities

When a person registers on the Accountable Humanity network, their identity is permanently anchored to approximate GPS coordinates captured during the in-person verification process. This geographic anchor cannot be changed after registration.

The coordinates are approximate — enough to confirm a general area, not precise enough to reveal an exact address. The goal is not surveillance but accountability: ensuring that identities cannot be falsely claimed in geographic contexts where they do not belong.

What This Prevents

Fake local reviews. A review site can verify that a reviewer is actually anchored to the geographic area of the business. Bot farms generating thousands of fake reviews from another country become detectable.

Astroturfed local politics. When a petition or public comment claims to represent local residents, platforms can verify that the signers are actually from the relevant area. Manufactured grassroots campaigns from outside actors become visible.

Foreign influence operations. Foreign actors masquerading as domestic voices is one of the most effective disinformation tactics. Geographic anchoring makes it possible to verify that a voice in a local conversation is actually local.

Privacy Preserved

Geographic anchoring does not reveal a person's exact location or address. The approximate coordinates confirm a general region — enough for a platform to verify "this reviewer is from the same metro area as this business" without exposing where the reviewer lives.

Combined with the network's pseudo-anonymous identity model, geographic accountability adds a layer of trust without compromising individual privacy.
